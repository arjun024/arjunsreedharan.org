You don’t have root access to your Google Nexus 4, which you spent your hard earned money to buy??

Don’t worry!!     

Rooting your Nexus 4 is but a piece of Cake. (Do at your own risk though!)

First things first – THE PROCEDURE BELOW WILL WIPE ALL YOUR DATA CLEAN.

1. Take a Backup of your personal files, contacts, messages etc (only if you want to :P).

 

2. Download Google Nexus 4 USB drivers (https://developers.google.com/android/nexus/drivers) and unzip it.

 

3. Turn on USB debugging mode from Developer Settings in your phone and connect it to your PC.

 

4. Go to Device Manager, select your phone, right click -> update drivers and when it asks for driver location, browse to the downloaded unzipped folder and select it. Driver install successful.

 

5. Download Android SDK, Team Win Recovery Project (twrp) image and Chainfire-SuperSU zip

                Links:     http://developer.android.com/sdk/index.html,

                                http://techerrata.com/browse/twrp2/mako,

                                http://download.chainfire.eu/344/SuperSU/

 

Android SDK gives you two main tools – adb and fastboot.

adb (Android debug bridge) is a client-server tool that lets you communicate with an Android device.

When you’re working in the bootloader, adb no longer works. You’re not yet booted into Android, and the debugging tools aren’t active to communicate with. That’s where we use fastboot.

fastboot is a utility that lets you directly flash the filesystem via USB.

Both of them are located in the /sdk/platform-tools directory of your android sdk­.

 

6. Go into the /sdk/platform-tools folder of Android SDK and locate adb and fastboot files

 

7. Copy the Team Win Project Recovery image file into there. (This is done for ease of typing commands in the command prompt). The image filename would be something like openrecovery-twrp-x.x.x.x-mako.img

 

#### Now we will proceed to unlock the bootloader ####

 

8. Make sure your phone is detected by adb. For that Open your browse via command line to the /sdk/platform-tools of your android sdk folder and type in:

   adb devices

   This should list your device if all is well.      

 

9. Now reboot your phone in the bootloader mode.

 adb reboot bootloader

10. Now to unlock the bootloader:

fastboot oem unlock

11. Confirm by selecting ‘Yes’ using Volume-Up and Power button.

 

 

#### Now we will flash the twrp custom recovery ####

 

12. Again reboot your phone in the bootloader mode

 

13. Flash twrp recovery.

fastboot flash recovery <name-of-twrp-recovery-image>

14. Select ‘Recovery mode' using volume buttons and Power button to get into twrp homescreen.

15. Select Backup and take a backup.

16. Again come to twrp homescreen

17. Reboot

 

######### Rooting ##########

18. Connect phone to PC via USB and transfer SuperSu zip to phone’s internal memory

19. Again reboot your phone in the bootloader mode

20. Select ‘Recovery mode’ to get into twrp homescreen

21. Select Install

22. Select the SuperSu zip file from its path

23. Click/Swipe on ‘Install’

24. Reboot

 

############## You are Now the Root ##############
